Numbers in miniscript are always stored in full-precision format (like the double type in C and C#). In other words, they can represent both whole numbers and decimals. The following are all valid MiniScript numbers:

42
-5
3.14157

They also represent true (1) and false (0), as well as degrees of truth in between (see fuzzy logic). The built-in keywords true and false are equivalent to 1 and 0, respectively.

Numbers support the following operators:

+, -, *, /add, subtract, multiply, divide
%mod (remainder)
^power
and, or, notlogical operators
==, !=, >, >=, <, <=comparison
